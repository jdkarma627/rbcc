# RBCC Skill Prompt Templates

This file provides reusable prompt patterns for the 12 active marketing skills in `.github/skills/`.
Use each section as a starter, then customize the bracketed inputs for the exact campaign or task.

## 1. product-marketing-context

**Use When**: Starting a new campaign or updating foundational positioning, audience, and proof points.

**Inputs**: mission, audience segments, differentiators, proof points, goals.

**Base Prompt**: Create an updated product marketing context for RBCC in Edmonton, including positioning for sponsors, players, and community members, core differentiators, and 2026 growth goals.

**Refinement Prompt**: Improve the context with stronger sponsor objections and responses, plus exact language from chats and briefing documents.

## 2. social-content

**Use When**: Building weekly or monthly social calendars and post drafts.

**Inputs**: platform, campaign goal, audience, event/story, CTA.

**Base Prompt**: Build a 4-week RBCC social calendar for [platform] with match highlights, player spotlights, sponsor appreciation, and community updates, each with hook, caption, and CTA.

**Refinement Prompt**: Rewrite week 1 for higher engagement by strengthening hooks, shortening captions, and adding platform-specific format guidance.

## 3. copywriting

**Use When**: Writing website, event, and sponsor copy that must convert.

**Inputs**: page type, target reader, one key message, primary CTA, proof.

**Base Prompt**: Write conversion-focused copy for RBCC [page type] targeting [audience], emphasizing [single value proposition] and ending with a clear [CTA].

**Refinement Prompt**: Produce 3 stronger headline options and tighten body copy to one core promise with less generic language.

## 4. paid-ads

**Use When**: Planning paid campaigns for membership, sponsorship, or event attendance.

**Inputs**: channel, audience, budget, timeline, landing page, KPI.

**Base Prompt**: Create a paid ads plan for RBCC on [channel] to drive [goal], including campaign structure, audience segments, messaging angles, budget split, and optimization steps.

**Refinement Prompt**: Split the plan into two test campaigns (community angle vs competitive angle) with week-1 learning criteria and next actions.

## 5. site-architecture

**Use When**: Defining or improving website structure and navigation.

**Inputs**: site goals, visitor types, key pages, conversion priorities.

**Base Prompt**: Propose RBCC website architecture with pages for About, Teams, Events, Sponsors, Gallery, News, and Contact, optimized for sponsor and membership conversions.

**Refinement Prompt**: Rework navigation to reduce clicks to sponsor inquiry and membership signup, and recommend URL structure and menu order.

## 6. sales-enablement

**Use When**: Creating sponsor-facing one-pagers, decks, and objection handling assets.

**Inputs**: sponsor tiers, deliverables, ROI proof, target buyer, objections.

**Base Prompt**: Draft a one-page RBCC sponsorship leave-behind covering tier options, benefits, proof points, and a clear next step for sponsor decision-makers.

**Refinement Prompt**: Add objection-response lines for budget and ROI concerns and tailor wording for local business owners.

## 7. analytics-tracking

**Use When**: Designing measurement plans for sponsor, membership, and event funnels.

**Inputs**: conversion events, channels, attribution needs, reporting cadence.

**Base Prompt**: Create an RBCC analytics tracking plan with event names, triggers, properties, and reporting views for sponsor inquiries, event registrations, and membership leads.

**Refinement Prompt**: Add UTM naming standards and define a weekly dashboard structure for channel attribution and conversion quality.

## 8. email-sequence

**Use When**: Building lifecycle or nurture email flows for sponsors, members, and attendees.

**Inputs**: segment, sequence goal, cadence, number of emails, CTA.

**Base Prompt**: Design a [number]-email RBCC sequence for [segment] to move them from [start state] to [target action], including objective and CTA per email.

**Refinement Prompt**: Segment the sequence by sponsorship tier or member type and adjust messaging by expected value and objections.

## 9. cold-email

**Use When**: Writing first-touch outreach to prospective sponsors and partners.

**Inputs**: prospect profile, outreach reason, proof point, low-friction ask.

**Base Prompt**: Write a concise RBCC cold email to [prospect] with a personalized opener, one relevant proof point, and a simple reply-based CTA.

**Refinement Prompt**: Increase personalization using prospect context and rewrite CTA to reduce friction while preserving intent.

## 10. marketing-psychology

**Use When**: Reframing messaging to improve decisions and conversions.

**Inputs**: target audience, hesitation point, current message, desired behavior.

**Base Prompt**: Apply marketing psychology to improve RBCC messaging for [audience], using social proof, anchoring, and loss aversion in an ethical way.

**Refinement Prompt**: Build a 3-stage message ladder (awareness, consideration, commitment) with specific wording for each stage.

## 11. competitor-alternatives

**Use When**: Creating fair comparison content against local alternatives.

**Inputs**: competitor list, RBCC strengths, audience, format.

**Base Prompt**: Draft RBCC competitor comparison content for [audience] that fairly presents alternatives and clearly explains RBCC fit and differentiation.

**Refinement Prompt**: Reframe to a positive RBCC-led narrative while keeping claims factual and verifiable.

## 12. page-cro

**Use When**: Improving underperforming pages with low conversion or high bounce.

**Inputs**: page goal, traffic source, current performance, known friction points.

**Base Prompt**: Audit RBCC [page] for conversion blockers and return top 3 changes by impact, including revised headline/CTA options.

**Refinement Prompt**: Propose A/B tests for headline and CTA variants with success metric, sample size guidance, and test duration.

## Usage Notes

- Start new campaigns with `product-marketing-context` when context has changed.
- Reuse winning prompts and record edits that improved output quality.
- Pair skills for full workflows, for example:
  - `site-architecture` + `copywriting` + `page-cro`
  - `sales-enablement` + `cold-email` + `analytics-tracking`
  - `social-content` + `paid-ads` + `email-sequence`
