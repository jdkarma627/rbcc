# RBCC Member Pitch Guide (v2)

## Purpose

Give every member a clear, repeatable way to introduce RBCC partnerships to local businesses without overpromising or freelancing terms.

## 30-Second Pitch

"We're Royal Bengal Cricket Club — Edmonton's reigning champions. We partner with local businesses for year-round advertising through our matches, events, and community reach. Everything is in writing, and the marketing work is handled by professionals. Can I send you our package?"

That's it. Keep it short. The goal is to open a conversation, not close a deal.

## First Conversation — What to Ask

Lead with questions, not a sales pitch. You want to understand their business before suggesting a tier.

1. "What would a successful partnership look like for your business?"
2. "Which matters more to you — brand awareness, community presence, or reaching specific professionals?"
3. "Do you have a rough annual budget in mind for local marketing like this?"
4. "Who on your team would handle the partnership if we move forward?"

Then close with: "I'll take this back to our team, and we'll put together a written proposal that fits what you described."

## What You Can Do

- Share the partner package document — it's ready to send as-is
- Walk through the three tiers and explain how each works
- Explain that marketing execution is handled by qualified professionals
- Collect their goals, budget range, and preferred contact

## What You Cannot Do

- Promise custom deliverables, pricing, or start dates
- Agree to exclusivity or category lock-outs
- Negotiate against other members or adjust tiers informally
- Commit to anything not in the standard package

All of the above go to Executive Review.

## Objection Handling

### "What exactly will I get?"

The real question is whether this is organized or ad hoc. Answer that directly.

"Everything is documented in a written agreement — the specific deliverables, timing, and how we'll report back to you. We don't commit to anything we can't deliver and prove."

### "Can you guarantee sales or foot traffic?"

The real question is whether their money will be wasted. Address the risk.

"We guarantee the visibility deliverables we agree to — and we provide evidence of each one. We can't promise specific sales numbers, but we can show you exactly where and how your brand appeared."

### "Can we start now and pay later?"

The real question is whether the commitment is flexible. Be clear without being rigid.

"We begin activation once the agreement is signed and first payment is received. We're happy to discuss a payment schedule that works for your cash flow."

### "How is this different from just putting up a banner?"

The real question is whether this has substance. This is your chance to differentiate.

"Our partners get year-round visibility across 52+ touchpoints — matches, tournaments, community events, social media, newsletters. All designed and reported by professionals. A banner is one touchpoint. This is twelve months of structured marketing."

### "We'd rather contribute services than cash."

"We're open to that. We have an in-kind partnership option where your contribution is matched to an equivalent tier for visibility. I can connect you with our team to scope that out."

## What NOT to Say

- "We'll do whatever you want" — you can't, and it undermines the structured package
- "This is a great deal" or "You won't find this anywhere else" — let the package speak for itself
- "Other sponsors are already lined up" — don't create fake urgency
- Any dollar-specific ROI promise ("You'll get $50K in exposure") — we report deliverables, not made-up valuations

Use this path for every request outside the standard package.
