# RBCC Brand Guide 2026 (v2)

## Purpose

This guide defines how Royal Bengal Cricket Club (RBCC) should look, sound, and present itself across sponsor materials, social media, event branding, and internal documents.

This is a working guide designed for immediate use by members creating outreach and marketing assets.

## Brand Position

RBCC is a competitive, community-based cricket club that offers local businesses a practical marketing and advertising channel into a high-trust, high-engagement audience.

### Professional Services Positioning

RBCC marketing materials, campaign design, and advertising execution are produced by qualified professionals. Members handle relationships, discovery, and community presence. Creative delivery, reporting, and campaign management meet professional standards — not volunteer guesswork.

When describing RBCC's partnership offering to prospects, always convey:

- Creative and campaign work is handled by professionals
- Reporting is structured and evidence-based
- The club runs on governance and accountability, not ad hoc effort

### Core Brand Principles

- Professional first: clean, consistent, and reliable presentation.
- Community connected: local, inclusive, and relationship-driven.
- Performance backed: claims tied to delivered actions and evidence.
- Under promise, over deliver: only commit what RBCC can execute.

## Voice and Messaging

### Voice Attributes

- Clear
- Direct
- Respectful
- Confident
- Accountable

### Default Messaging Frame

Use: "marketing and advertising partnership"

Avoid: donation-first or vague sponsorship language with no deliverables.

### Approved Language

- "Year-over-year partnership"
- "Defined deliverables"
- "Measured visibility"
- "Activation starts after payment is received"
- "Custom requests are finalized through written agreement"

### Language to Avoid

- "We can do anything you want"
- "Guaranteed sales"
- "Unlimited exposure"
- Any promise without owner, frequency, and proof method

## Banned Words

Do not use these words in any sponsor-facing or public material. They signal generic marketing copy and erode trust.

- leverage / leveraging
- unlock / unlocking
- empower / empowering
- innovative / innovation
- streamline / streamlining
- cutting-edge
- game-changer / game-changing
- holistic
- synergy / synergize
- robust
- revolutionize / revolutionary
- transformative / transform (when used as hype)
- utilize (use "use" instead)
- facilitate (use "handle" or "run" instead)
- spearhead (use "lead" or "run" instead)
- fostering (use "advancing," "promoting," or "building" instead)
- best-in-class
- world-class (unless literally true and provable)
- next-level
- end-to-end (use a specific description instead)
- seamless (use "smooth" or describe what actually happens)

Also avoid:
- Emoji in headings or section titles
- Exclamation points in professional documents
- Blockquote-styled hype lines ("We're not just a club — we're a movement")
- Unattributed superlatives ("the best," "the most," "the only" — unless verifiable)

## Logo Usage

Current source file: Marketing_Plans/RBCC_Logo.png

### Rules

- Maintain original logo proportions. Do not stretch or distort.
- Keep clear space around logo equal to at least the height of the letter "R" in the logo.
- Use full-color logo on light backgrounds when contrast is strong.
- Use monochrome or reversed versions on complex/dark backgrounds.
- Do not add effects (shadow, glow, outline, gradients) to logo.

### Minimum Size

- Digital: 120 px width minimum
- Print: 30 mm width minimum

## Typography

### Recommended Fonts

- Headings: Montserrat Bold / Semibold
- Body: Lato Regular / Semibold
- Fallback: Arial, sans-serif

### Font Sources

Google Fonts imports:

```
https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Montserrat:wght@600;700&family=Lato:wght@400;600&display=swap
```

### Weight Mappings

- Headings: Montserrat 600 (Semibold) for H2–H3, 700 (Bold) for H1
- Body: Lato 400 (Regular) for text, 600 (Semibold) for emphasis and labels
- Do not use weights below 400 for any text

### Type Scale

- H1: 36 px
- H2: 28 px
- H3: 22 px
- Body large: 18 px
- Body: 16 px
- Caption: 13 px

### Spacing

- Heading line-height: 1.2
- Body line-height: 1.6
- Heading letter-spacing: -0.02em
- Body letter-spacing: normal

### Rules

- Keep headings short and specific.
- Prefer sentence case over all caps.
- Do not use more than two font families in a single asset.

## Visual Style

### Photography Direction

- Use real RBCC moments: matches, training, events, volunteers, family/community engagement.
- Prefer bright, high-contrast photos with clear subjects.
- Avoid low-quality screenshots and blurry images.
- For proof-of-concept or draft materials where photos are not yet available, use solid brand-color backgrounds or clean geometric patterns instead of stock photography. Never use generic stock cricket images.

### Graphic Style

- Clean layouts with generous spacing.
- One core message per section.
- Keep sponsor logos readable; do not crowd logos.

## Sponsor Co-Branding Rules

- RBCC logo and sponsor logo must have balanced visual weight.
- Do not place sponsor logo smaller than 70% of RBCC logo height in joint headers.
- Use neutral or brand-safe backgrounds to keep both logos readable.
- For multi-sponsor placements, follow tier order and equal spacing within tier.

## Approval Workflow

### What Members Can Do Without Extra Approval

- Use approved templates and approved deliverables language.
- Send sponsor package and pitch guide as-is.

### What Requires Exec Approval

- Custom sponsor requests not in package
- Price exceptions
- New logo treatments
- Exclusive category requests

## Quick Compliance Checklist

Before sharing any sponsor-facing material, confirm:

1. Uses marketing/advertising partnership language.
2. Uses approved colors and fonts.
3. Includes only approved deliverables.
4. Includes payment and written agreement requirement.
5. Contains no unverifiable performance claims.
